# Coupon Acceptance Analysis: Strategic Behavior Report

This report highlights the findings from an investigation into customer behavior regarding mobile coupon acceptance. By analyzing over 12,000 survey responses, we have identified the specific "sweet spots" where marketing efforts are most effective.

## Executive Summary
Overall, **56.9%** of coupons were accepted. However, success seems to be dependent on  **Habit, Timing, Coupon type, and Social Context.**

---

## Detailed Findings: The "Sweet Spots"

### 1. The Power of Habit (Bar Coupons)
The strongest predictor of acceptance is a customer's existing lifestyle.
* **Frequent Visitors:** Customers who visit bars more than 3 times a month accept coupons at a **76.2%** rate.
* **Infrequent Visitors:** Those who visit once or never accept at only **37.3%**.
* **The "Social" Driver:** Drivers under 30 or those traveling with friends/partners are significantly more likely to accept a detour to a bar than those with children or those driving alone.

### 2. Timing vs. Expiration (Cheap Restaurants)
For coupons under $20, the window of opportunity is narrow:
* **The Golden Window:** Acceptance is highest (**80%+**) between **2 PM and 6 PM**, but *mostly* if the coupon has a **1-day expiration**.
* **The Low Point:** Acceptance drops significantly for short-duration (2-hour) coupons sent in the early morning (7 AM) or late night (10 PM).

### 3. Education & Occupational Trends (Carry-Out)
Analysis of "Carry Out & Take Away" coupons revealed surprising demographic trends:
* **The "High School" Segment:** Customers with "Some High School" education showed a near **100% acceptance rate** across almost all times of day, except for 10AM.
* **Selective Professionals:** Graduate degree holders and high-income earners are much more selective, only showing high interest during the mid-afternoon (2 PM - 6 PM) window.

---

## Strategic Recommendations
1.  **Extend Expirations:** For restaurant coupons, moving from a 2-hour to a 1-day expiration significantly increases the conversion rate during the late afternoon.
2.  **Segment by Education:** Carry-out campaigns should prioritize lower-education and student segments, as they show nearly universal acceptance regardless of the time.
3.  **Social-Proofing:** Bar and restaurant coupons should be triggered specifically when the driver is detected to be a frequent establishment visitor.
